A sound can trigger a lot in your mind, and if it is a human sound, you begin to infer - do you know the person, what is s/he saying, the personality, health status, etc. Drawn by the information packed nature of sound signals, Project Coswara is launched to evaluate the effectiveness in COVID-19 diagnosis using respiratory sound samples. The idea is to collect and analyze breath, cough, and speech samples use using digital technology for detecting respiratory ailments including COVID-19.

  • Reasoning: Speech breathing patterns are intricately tied to changes in anatomy and physiology of the respiratory system. The WHO and the CDC have listed dry cough, difficulty in breathing, and chest pain as key symptoms of COVID-19, visible between 2-14 days after exposure to the virus.
  • Goal: a) To publish a large dataset of respiratory sound samples, (b) design signal processing and machine learning techniques to analyze and detect respiratory infections, and (c) augment expertise from doctors for remote diagnosis of respiratory infections using respiratory sound samples.
  • Note: The goal is not to replace the existing COVID-19 chemical testing methodologies but to supplement them with cost effective, fast and simpler techniques for pre-screening of Covid-19.

This blog will share our journey in this project. We will try to make posts as and when we find something interesting to share from this work.
